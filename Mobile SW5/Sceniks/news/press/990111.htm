SCENIX SEMICONDUCTOR NAMES BULENT CELEBI AS PRESIDENT AND CEO

Analog Devices General Manager Exits To Head Innovative MCU Supplier
Santa Clara, Calif. - Jan. 11, 1999 - Scenix Semiconductor, Inc. today announced that Bulent Celebi, 43, has been named president and chief executive officer (CEO), with overall responsibility for all aspects of the company's operations, including strategic positioning, product definition and development, sales and marketing, foundry relationships and financing. Steve Leung, who has served as president and CEO since co-founding the company, remains as Chairman of the Board of Directors.
Mr. Celebi, who has served on Scenix Semiconductor's board since the company's formation and was involved in its product definition and strategy, brings almost 20 years of experience in engineering, marketing and general management to his new role. Throughout his career, he has successfully built market share and profitability for semiconductor suppliers providing LAN (local area network) and telecommunications products, analog integrated circuits and embedded controllers by creating and executing strategies that leverage corporate strengths and capabilities to increase design wins and customer loyalty.
"Mr. Celebi brings strengths and experience that will be needed to keep Scenix on the growth path as its sales rapidly ramp up," said Steve Leung, Chairman. "He has the proven ability to successfully position a company in a new, high-growth market, and is the right person at the right time to help us achieve our goal of being the industry leader in 8-bit MCUs."
"Scenix has some of the industry's leading microcontroller technology, and its Virtual Peripheral approach to implementing functions in software is redefining the market," Mr. Celebi said. "The company is positioned to become a major supplier to the embedded controller industry, especially in the growing communications and Internet connectivity markets. I'm extremely pleased to have the opportunity to help direct that movement. My goal is to establish Scenix as the industry standard in 8-bit microcontrollers based on both its superior technology and its commitment to total customer support."
Before joining Scenix, Mr. Celebi was with Analog Devices, Inc., for 2 years, most recently as vice president and general manager of the product lines based in Santa Clara, Calif., which included power management, temperature sensor, amplifier and aerospace products. He was also responsible for directing Analog Devices' recent and dramatic penetration of the personal computer market.
Previously, Mr. Celebi was with National Semiconductor for nearly 10 years. During that time, he served as general manager and business unit manager of the Microcontroller Business Unit in Santa Clara, Calif., director of product management in the LAN Division, director of marketing in the Communications & Computing Group, and director of marketing for the Southeast Asia Division. His experience also includes marketing manager for Philips/Signetics in Santa Clara, Calif., and chief engineer in the Digital Division of Monitek/PCEC in Hayward, Calif.
Mr. Celebi attended the University of California, Berkeley, majoring in electrical engineering and computer science.
Scenix Semiconductor, Inc. is headquartered in Santa Clara, Calif. Founded in 1996, the company's mission is to deliver high-performance, cost-effective, easy-to-use single-chip solutions for embedded systems. Scenix announced the 50 MIPS SX Series family of 8-bit microcontrollers in August 1997 and shipped the first production parts in late December 1997. In October 1998, the company announced the 100 MIPS members of the SX Series, and in December 1998, Cahners Publishing placed the SX Series on its "10 Top Processor List of 1998."
